The old lighthouse at the edge of Dunmore had been dark for thirty years. No one went near it — not since the night the keeper disappeared without a trace. But on a stormy Tuesday in October, sixteen-year-old Sam found a rusted key wedged beneath the door of the town library with a note that simply read: "Go before midnight." Sam had two choices: head to the lighthouse alone, or call a friend.

Sam's thumb hovered over the screen before finally pressing call. "Jess, I know it's late — but I need

  ▎ key warm in Sam's palm. The path wound along the cliffs, and with every step the wind grew louder, as if
  ▎ the sea itself was warning them back. The lighthouse door swung open at the touch of the key — it

  ▎ touch, and beside it a logbook opened to the final entry: the keeper's handwriting, dated that very
  ▎ night, thirty years ago, reading simply: "She's coming back."
