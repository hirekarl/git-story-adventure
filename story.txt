The old lighthouse at the edge of Dunmore had been dark for thirty years. No one went near it — not since the night the keeper disappeared without a trace. But on a stormy Tuesday in October, sixteen-year-old Sam found a rusted key wedged beneath the door of the town library with a note that simply read: "Go before midnight." Sam had two choices: head to the lighthouse alone, or call a friend.

Sam pocketed the key and stepped out into the rain alone. The path to the lighthouse wound along the cliffs, and with every step the wind grew louder, as if the sea itself was warning her back. The lighthouse door swung open at the touch of the key — it hadn't been locked at all. Inside, on the spiral staircase, someone had left a lantern still warm to the touch, and beside it a logbook opened to the final entry: the keeper's handwriting, dated that very night, thirty years ago, reading simply: "She's coming back."
