The old lighthouse at the edge of Dunmore had been dark for thirty years. No one went near it — not since the night the keeper disappeared without a trace. But on a stormy Tuesday in October, sixteen-year-old Sam found a rusted key wedged beneath the door of the town library with a note that simply read: "Go before midnight." Sam had two choices: head to the lighthouse alone, or call a friend.

Sam's thumb hovered over the screen before finally pressing call. "Jess, I know it's late — but I need you," Sam whispered. Jess arrived in twelve minutes, rain-soaked and wide-eyed, insisting they go together or not at all. The two of them crossed the rocky path toward the lighthouse side by side, the key warm in Sam's palm. Whatever waited at the top, at least neither of them would face it alone.
